AI Receptionist FAQ: Real Answers to the Questions People Actually Ask

No marketing spin — the three questions people search before they buy, answered directly.

Can an AI receptionist really answer all my business calls?

Yes, for the calls that make up the vast majority of small-business phone volume — hours, availability, pricing, booking, general questions. It answers every call instantly, 24/7, with no busy signal and no hold queue, because it isn't one line that gets tied up the way a single human receptionist would be.

The honest limit: for a call it genuinely can't resolve — a request outside its knowledge base, or something that needs real human judgment — it doesn't just drop the call. It takes a detailed message, offers a callback, or transfers to your business line, depending on how you configure it. Nothing falls through silently; every call gets logged either way, so you always know it happened.

How much does an AI receptionist cost vs. hiring staff?

A part-time receptionist at $15–20/hr for 20 hours a week runs roughly $1,200–$1,600/month in wages alone — before payroll tax, benefits, training time, or covering sick days and turnover. And that only buys part-time coverage, not 24/7.

Will customers notice they're talking to an AI receptionist?

Yes — and that's intentional, not something we try to hide. Every call opens with a clear, spoken disclosure that it's an AI assistant, so there's no attempt to pass it off as human.

In practice, most callers care far more about getting a fast, accurate answer than about who or what is answering. The professionalism concern usually resolves itself once you hear the actual call quality — a well-built voice AI sounds natural, doesn't put people on hold, and answers consistently every single time, which most human-staffed lines can't promise at every hour of the day.
