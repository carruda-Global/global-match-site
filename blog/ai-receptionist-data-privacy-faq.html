How Secure Is an AI Receptionist with Customer Data?

Privacy and security concerns, answered directly — not with vague reassurance.

This is a question worth pushing every vendor on directly, since "secure" without specifics is a marketing word, not an answer. Here's what to actually check.

The four things to verify with any vendor

QuestionWhy it matters
Is recording disclosed to callers?Legally required in many states — undisclosed recording carries real penalties
How is call data stored?Encrypted, managed infrastructure vs. an unclear or unspecified setup
How long is data retained?Indefinite retention is a bigger liability surface than a fixed, short window
Can a caller request deletion?Relevant for privacy-law compliance depending on your market
AION's answers, specifically: every call discloses recording upfront, transcripts and recordings are purged after 90 days automatically, and call data is stored on encrypted, managed infrastructure — not a plain server file.

Why this matters beyond compliance

Beyond the legal requirement, how a vendor handles this is a reasonable signal for how seriously they take the rest of the product. A vendor vague about data handling is worth being cautious about elsewhere too.
