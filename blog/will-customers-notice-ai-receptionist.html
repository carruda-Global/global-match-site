Will Customers Notice They're Talking to an AI Receptionist?

Yes — and that's intentional, not a flaw to hide.

This is usually the second concern right after "does it actually work" — and the honest answer is that customers will notice, because every call is required to disclose it upfront.

Why disclosure isn't optional

Every AION call opens with a clear, spoken statement that the caller is talking to an AI assistant, and that the call may be recorded and analyzed. That's not a design choice you can turn off — several states carry criminal penalties for undisclosed call recording, so disclosure is built in for every business on the platform, in every market. There's no version of this where a caller is tricked into thinking they're talking to a human.

What actually shapes customer perception

In our experience the disclosure itself rarely bothers callers — what shapes perception is call quality. A caller who gets an instant answer, a natural-sounding voice, and their question actually resolved perceives the interaction as professional, disclosure and all. A caller who hits a busy signal, a long hold, or an answering machine perceives that as unprofessional, no AI required.
