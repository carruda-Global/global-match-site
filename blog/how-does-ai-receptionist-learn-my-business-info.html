How Does an AI Receptionist Know My Business Information?

Setup and data integration, explained plainly.

An AI receptionist doesn't inherently know anything about your business — it knows exactly what you tell it, in the same way a brand-new human hire would need to be onboarded before their first shift.

Where the information actually comes from

  • Direct configuration — you enter hours, pricing, services, and common Q&A during setup
  • Website reference — some platforms can pull baseline details from your existing site to speed up setup, which you then review and correct
  • Live integrations — for things like real-time appointment availability, it connects to your actual calendar rather than working off a static snapshot

Keeping it accurate over time

This is the part that's easy to overlook: an AI receptionist configured once and never touched again will start giving outdated answers the moment your hours, prices, or services change. Treat the configuration the way you'd treat a website — something that needs updating when the underlying facts change, not a one-time setup you forget about.

Practical habit: whenever you'd update your Google Business listing or website with a change, update your AI receptionist's configuration at the same time. They should never drift out of sync.

This connects directly to how setup actually works and what information it can reliably give callers once it's configured correctly.
