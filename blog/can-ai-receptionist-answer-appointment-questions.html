Can an AI Receptionist Answer Questions About Appointments?

Sharing availability, confirming bookings, and handling reschedules.

This is the natural follow-up to booking — once an appointment exists, callers frequently call back to confirm, change, or cancel it, and that's just as automatable as the original booking.

What this looks like in practice

  • Confirmation — "Is my appointment still on for Thursday at 2pm?" answered by looking up the caller's existing booking
  • Rescheduling — checking new availability and moving the slot, live, during the call
  • Cancellation — processing a cancellation and, depending on your policy, capturing the reason
  • General availability — "Do you have anything open this week?" without a booking already on file

The same integration requirement as booking

Just like new appointment booking, this depends on a live connection to your actual scheduling system rather than a static script. Without that integration, the AI can still capture the request and pass it along for manual handling — slower, but still faster than a missed call. Why this matters more than it seems: appointment-related calls are often the single highest-volume category for service businesses — dental, medical, salons, and home services all live and die on scheduling call volume.
