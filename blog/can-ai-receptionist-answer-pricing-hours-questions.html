Can an AI Receptionist Handle Customer Questions About Pricing and Hours?

What information can it actually provide to callers?

This is one of the highest-frequency, lowest-risk things an AI receptionist does — and it's worth understanding exactly what "can it answer pricing questions" really means in practice.

What it handles reliably

  • Business hours, including holiday hours if you keep it updated
  • Flat-rate pricing, starting prices, or published rate cards
  • Service area and location details
  • Standard policies — cancellation windows, deposits, what's included

This is information you'd otherwise have staff repeating dozens of times a day, or callers hunting for on a website that may not have it. Making it available instantly over the phone removes a small but constant source of friction.

Where it needs a handoff instead

Not all pricing is static. A custom estimate for a home renovation, a medical procedure cost that depends on insurance, or a quote that genuinely requires seeing the job first — a well-built AI receptionist recognizes these as "I can't give you an exact number" situations and captures the caller's details for a real quote, rather than fabricating one. That distinction matters: an AI confidently guessing at a quote is worse than one that says "let me get you an exact number from our team."

Keeping information accurate is on you, not the AI. If your hours or prices change, update the AI's configuration the same day — see how an AI receptionist learns your business information for how that update process actually works.
