Can I Customize What an AI Receptionist Says?

Personalization and branding, and where the limits are.

Customization is one of the more underused levers business owners have — many treat the default script as fixed when most of it is actually editable.

What you can typically customize

  • The greeting — how it opens the call and introduces your business
  • Tone and formality — see training it to sound professional for how this affects perception
  • What it emphasizes — a seasonal promotion, a new service, or a policy you want every caller to hear
  • How it phrases escalations — the words used when handing off to a human

What should stay fixed

The AI disclosure at the start of every call — the statement that callers are talking to an AI assistant and the call may be recorded — is a legal requirement in many states and shouldn't be edited out, even if it feels like it slows down the greeting. This isn't a limitation specific to any vendor; it's a compliance floor every business on any platform should respect.

Where to start customizing: your greeting and your most common 5-10 questions. Those cover the overwhelming majority of calls, so getting them right in your own voice matters more than polishing edge cases first.

Once you've customized the script, the next step is reviewing it against real calls — see listening to call recordings for how to catch what needs adjusting.
