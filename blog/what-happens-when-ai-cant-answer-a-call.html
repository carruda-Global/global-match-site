What Happens When an AI Receptionist Can't Answer a Call?

How does it handle complex issues or escalations?

The honest concern behind this question isn't "does the AI ever fail" — it's "what happens to the caller when it does." That's the part worth actually understanding before you hand over your phone line.

The three escalation paths

  • Live transfer — the call routes to your business line or a designated staff member in real time, for anything urgent
  • Detailed message — the AI captures exactly what the caller needs, along with contact info, so a callback isn't a guessing game
  • Scheduled callback — the caller is told when to expect a call back, instead of being left wondering

You configure which path fits which situation. A caller asking a complex billing dispute might trigger a live transfer during business hours; the same call at 11pm might trigger a callback offer instead.

Why this matters more than the raw "can it answer everything" question

No system — human or AI — resolves 100% of calls without ever needing to hand off. The difference between a good and bad escalation path isn't whether handoffs happen, it's whether the caller experiences a dead end or a clear next step. See how customers reach a human when the AI can't help for the caller-side view of this same process.

Nothing gets lost silently. Every call — resolved, transferred, or logged as a message — is recorded in your dashboard, so you always have a full record even on the calls the AI didn't fully close out itself.

This is also the same logic that governs what happens if the AI makes a mistake — errors get caught and handed to a human, not buried.
