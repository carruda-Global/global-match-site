Can I Cancel an AI Receptionist Service Anytime?

Contract terms and flexibility vary a lot by vendor — here's what to actually check.

Contract terms are one of the least-advertised, most-important things to check before committing to any AI receptionist — the pricing page rarely tells you the whole story.

What to actually ask before signing up

  • Is there an annual contract, or is it month-to-month? Some vendors quote a lower monthly rate that only applies with an annual commitment
  • Is there an early termination fee? Some contracts penalize cancelling before a term ends
  • What happens to your phone number? If the vendor assigned you a new number rather than forwarding your existing one, cancelling could mean losing it
  • How much notice is required to cancel? Some require 30 days' written notice rather than an immediate self-service cancel
AION's terms: no annual contract, no lock-in — cancel whenever you want. There's also a 15-day free trial so you can evaluate real call quality before committing to a paid plan at all.

Why flexibility matters more than it seems upfront

Nobody signs up for an AI receptionist planning to cancel — but the businesses that most need flexibility are the ones testing whether this fits their specific call pattern in the first place. A vendor that requires a long commitment before you've proven it works for your business is asking you to take on risk that a month-to-month or trial-based vendor doesn't require.
