Can an AI Receptionist Answer Frequently Asked Questions?

FAQ automation capabilities, and how to set it up well.

FAQ handling is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-effort things to set up well, because it directly replaces one of the most repetitive parts of any front-desk job.

Why this beats a website FAQ page

A website FAQ only helps callers who think to look for it — many callers just pick up the phone instead. An AI receptionist puts the same information one spoken question away, with no searching, scrolling, or guessing which FAQ heading covers their question.

How to build a genuinely useful FAQ set

  • Start from real questions — pull from actual call logs, staff memory, or common emails, not a generic industry template
  • Prioritize frequency — the 10-15 questions that cover 80% of your call volume matter far more than covering every possible edge case
  • Keep answers current — an outdated FAQ answer is worse than no answer at all; see how it learns your business info for the update process
Common mistake: copying a generic industry FAQ list instead of your actual callers' questions. Two dental clinics in different neighborhoods can have completely different top-10 caller questions — build yours from your own data, not a template.

This pairs directly with handling pricing and hours questions, which is usually the largest single FAQ category for most small businesses.
