Is an AI Receptionist Better Than Hiring a Part-Time Receptionist?

Cost and capability comparison, without picking a side unfairly.

This comparison comes down to what specific job you actually need done — "answer the phone" and "run the front desk" are related but different jobs, and the two options aren't perfect substitutes for each other.

Where the AI receptionist wins clearly

  • Cost — $89/mo flat vs. $1,200+/mo for 20 hours/week of part-time wages, before payroll tax and benefits
  • Coverage hours — 24/7 vs. whatever shift a part-time hire works
  • Consistency — no sick days, no turnover, no bad-mood days affecting call quality
  • Concurrent calls — handles simultaneous calls a single part-time employee physically can't

Where a part-time human hire genuinely wins

  • In-person tasks — greeting visitors, handling physical mail or deliveries, managing a waiting room
  • Complex judgment calls that go beyond a configured script
  • Building ongoing personal relationships with regular, repeat callers
The realistic conclusion for most small businesses: an AI receptionist replaces the phone-answering portion of the role at a fraction of the cost, while a part-time hire — if you still need one — can focus on the parts that genuinely need a physical, in-person presence.
